Biography

Sundar Pichai, born on June 10, 1972, in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India, is a prominent technology executive known for his role as the CEO of Google and its parent company, Alphabet Inc. Pichai studied Metallurgical Engineering at IIT Kharagpur before earning an M.S. from Stanford University and an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. He joined Google in 2004 as a product manager, where he initially worked on the Google Toolbar and later led the development of Google Chrome, which launched in 2008 and became the most widely used web browser in the world. Pichai steadily rose through the ranks, becoming Senior Vice President in 2012, overseeing Chrome and Apps, and later the Product Chief in 2014. In 2015, he was appointed CEO of Google, and in 2019, he became the CEO of Alphabet Inc. Under his leadership, Google has advanced significantly in artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and hardware development while also promoting diversity and engaging in public discussions on technology ethics and privacy.

Achivements

  • Joined Google (2004): Started as a product manager and led the development of Google Toolbar and later Google Chrome.
  • CEO of Google (2015): Appointed CEO, overseeing major products like Search, Ads, Maps, and Android.
  • CEO of Alphabet Inc. (2019): Took on the role of CEO for Google's parent company, Alphabet Inc.
  • Advancements in AI and Cloud Computing: Led significant advancements in artificial intelligence and expanded Google Cloud services.
  • Hardware Innovations: Oversaw the launch of Google hardware products such as Pixel phones and Google Home.
  • Advocate for Diversity and Public Issues: Promoted diversity and inclusion within Google and actively participated in public discussions on privacy and AI ethics.
